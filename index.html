Connect24

Helping the Class of 2024 build resilience in a time of uncertainty and isolation



Introduction

Introduction



Problem Statement

Due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, the upcoming fall semester at Wellesley College will provide new challenges for many incoming freshmen.


Our Solution

Connect24 aims to help incoming students cope with an unusual fall semester by providing them with a means for connection with peers and upperclassmen, as well as improving the ease of involvement in extracurriculars

User analysis


User Interviews

We first conducted interviews with our user population, incoming college students, to identify their thoughts and concerns regarding the upcoming fall semester.


Interview #1
The interviewee was primarily concerned with the potential loss of connection and not being able to experience a “typical first semester”. Her main obstacles and frustrations were not being able to make friends and acquaintances, not being able to live on campus, and not being able to join organizations or be involved on campus.

Interview #2
The interviewee wants the “normal college experience” and admits that living at home would not be ideal. In the event that social interactions are limited to virtual meetings, she is worried that it will be more difficult to meet and befriend her classmates. She says online interactions can be “awkward” for people like her who aren’t accustomed to initiating conversations with strangers. She also desires to get involved in the community at Wellesley.

Interview #3
The interviewee feels that the social aspect of would not be “complete”, especially as she thinks it may not be as connected as other orientations she has heard of in the past. She is also concerned with not having many connections until class starts in person, which could be months away. An ideal situation for her would be an online experience as close to the normal experience as possible, with students being as active as they probably would be in person. She hopes to join organizations related to politics, art, or casual sport.

Ideation


Scenarios

We first conducted interviews with our user population, incoming college students, to identify their thoughts and concerns regarding the upcoming fall semester.

Scenario 1:
Anna, 18, is an incoming freshman at Wellesley College. She was heavily involved in her high school’s political club and debate team, and is hoping to continue similar activities on campus as well as explore new organizations. As an extroverted and outgoing person, she was initially excited about the possibility of making many new friends on campus. However, the potential remote semester causes her to worry about having less opportunities to connect when she reaches campus, or even not having opportunities at all due to remote instruction and time zone differences. Regardless of location status, she wants to have the ability to connect with other incoming freshmen and begin forming friendships. She especially wants to use the website as a platform for easy connection to other students with similar interests within her school, rather than seeking out individuals on the Facebook page.

Scenario 2:
Brittany is a highly creative and reserved incoming student. Throughout high school she was involved in many artistic and creative endeavors, including community outreach and freelancing. She looks forward to starting at Wellesley in the fall and plans on majoring in English or Art. Contributing to her community is extremely important to Brittany. She seeks to find purpose by using her creative skills to help those around her. She also seeks community through various extracurriculars and shared interests. Brittany is ready to get involved at Wellesley, but is overwhelmed by the sheer number of organizations. She knows that she should commit to only a few orgs so her passion is not divided. She is open to exploring several orgs before deciding which ones to commit her four undergraduate years to.

Scenario 3:
Brittany is an incoming first year at Wellesley College. She is not the most active on social media, and typically communicates with friends via text. As an introvert, she finds it difficult to open up to strangers, making it harder to find new friends. She has some anxiety about starting in the fall, and the possibility of a remote fall semester only worsens this stress since she won’t be able to connect with people who could support her. She seeks to connect with a big, Wellesley tradition in which underclassmen are paired with upperclassmen who offer support and mentorship. She hopes that finding a big will ease her transition since she will be able to connect with someone and ask questions. She hopes to find a big who shares her interest and understands her personality and communication style.


Design Directions and Rationale

We explored the following two preliminary design directions:


Design Direction #1:




Design 1 features a dashboard that acts as a community hub for personalized events and messages. The design uses survey information to connect the user with class members and organizations. It also provides the user with easy navigation to all the app's features. The user can conveniently access the dashboard by pressing the home button in the top right corner. 



Design Direction #2:



Design 2 features simple onboarding screens for learnability. It allows the user to efficiently switch between tabs, creating minimal steps for navigation and allowing for memorability. The three tabs clearly correspond to the three main functions of the app: connect with classmates, find a Big, and explore the Orgs. The design eliminates any unnecessary frills with a minimalist interface.  



Takeaways and Feedback

These are our main takeaways and insights from our in-class design presentation/critique.

People liked...
  • Community dashboard
  • Ability to favorite orgs
  • Starting tutorial promotes good learnability
  • Nav bar - convenient and minimal
  • Simple and friendly design
Suggestions for improvement:
  • More in-app interaction between students/bigs/orgs
  • Allowing students to ‘follow’ orgs to see updates from them
  • Match students to virtual roommates - give option in survey
  • In-app messaging feature
  • RSVP feature and allowing org leaders to edit/update information

Design Rationale:

  • Interface: Minimal yet engaging for easy use by the members of the incoming class. Incorporates elements from existing social media for learnability, such as a dashboard, a simple navigation bar, and a scrollable page of profiles.
  • Color scheme: Monochromatic color scheme, with red - the 2024 class color - as the primary color. The scheme was chosen to be minimal and aesthetically cohesive while establishing familiarity with the incoming class.
  • Graphic Design: Simple and minimalistic icons and navigation, to avoid visual clutter. Subtle boxes in muted colors group like items together, like classmates.
  • Typography: SF Pro, the system font for iOS, provides external consistency and legibility.


Heuristic Evaluation Takeaways

Overall, our evaluators liked the simple and minimalistic interface. They also liked the ease of navigation present in the app, and the aesthetically pleasing and fitting design.

One common critique is that the needs to be distinct separations between the saved contacts and the entire class of 2024. The "Classmates" and "Saved Contacts" are both accessed by heart icons, which caused confusion for the evaluators. We would fix this by haing separate locations for saved contacts and the general classmate browsing feature. The placement of the inbox button on the messaging page also caused confusion, which we will address by altering in a future iteration.

Another takeaway from the evaluations is that users need a way to edit the basic information they place in their questionnaire, due to events such as a mustake or change of pronouns. We will fix this by allowing users to alter their profiles by easily changing their inputs.

Some other elements mentioned in the heuristic evaluations: ensuring the vanigation bar is consistent acorss all pages; making the process of sending a message to a user more efficient; allowing the user to stay on the messaging page after sending a message; and adding help documentation to the app.

Takeaways and Future Iterations

We first want to focus on fixing certain features mentioned in the heuristic design evaluation to make the user experience more seamless. These include:

  • Accounting for every single action the user could take on the app and creating a more comprehensive prototype that is fully clickable and seamless.
  • Changing some icons to eliminate confusion over the two separate heart icons (classmates and saved contacts)

We then want to enhance the application by adding more engaging features, such as:

  • Allowing organizations to create an account and update/add information about their orgs. Students would also be able to message organizations in app rather than the current function which is external email.
